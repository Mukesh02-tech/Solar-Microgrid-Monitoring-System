This project proposes a highly efficient, cost-effective row-level monitoring and power management system for a solar microgrid. Designed to bridge the gap between array-level and panel-level monitoring, the system architecture utilizes a dual-end sensor placement strategy—featuring Light Dependent Resistors (LDRs) and temperature sensors at both ends of each row—to accurately detect uneven sunlight, partial shading, and thermal hotspots. Concurrently, real-time electrical parameters are measured by a central processing unit to compute row-wise power output and isolate specific faults. Furthermore, the system incorporates a vital energy management module to continuously monitor battery voltage. By analyzing this data alongside the solar array output, the microcontroller can automatically detect low power generation conditions and initiate a seamless power-shift to a backup source, ensuring an uninterrupted power supply. This integrated design provides superior fault localization and dynamic load management, offering a scalable and robust solution for optimizing solar microgrid performance.
